WPL 2026 retention: All players retained and released - full list
Smriti Mandhana and Harmanpreet Kaur were among the big names to be retained by their respective teams while Deepti Sharma and Alyssa Healy were released.
By Ali Asgar Nalwala
06 November 2025 18:32 GMT+5
The list of retained and released players by each team ahead of the Women’s Premier League (WPL) mega auction included plenty of surprises on Thursday.

While Smriti Mandhana, Harmanpreet Kaur, Shafali Verma and Jemimah Rodrigues were retained by their respective teams, Deepti Sharma, the Player of the Tournament at the Women’s ODI World Cup 2025, was released by UP Warriorz.

While most of the cricket teams executed their WPL 2026 retention strategies to maintain a balanced core, UP Warriorz looked to start afresh with just one retained player.

Each team could retain up to five players - three Indian capped players and either two overseas or two uncapped Indian players. In case a team opted to retain five players, at least one of them had to be an uncapped Indian.

Teams that retained fewer players will have more Right-to-Match (RTM) options and a larger purse going into the mega auction later this month.

Defending champions Mumbai Indians, led by Indian captain Harmanpreet Kaur, and three-time runners-up Delhi Capitals, who kept Shafali Verma and Jemimah Rodrigues in the side, have retained five players. At the mega auction, they will have no RTM options available.

UP Warriorz, who claimed the wooden spoon last season, went for a contrasting approach with a major overhaul. They retained the uncapped Shweta Sehrawat and will have the largest purse along with four RTMs at the auction to make a squad of at least 16 and up to 18 players.

Gujarat Giants, meanwhile, let go of South Africa’s Laura Wolvaardt, who was the top run-scorer at the Women’s World Cup 2025.

Australia's Alyssa Healy and Meg Lanning, New Zealand’s Amelia Kerr and South Africa’s Nadine de Klerk were some of the other notable names missing from the retention lists.
