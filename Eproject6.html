Squash World Cup 2025: India among 12 nations confirmed for mixed-team event in Chennai
Defending champions Egypt and last edition’s runners-up Malaysia also confirmed their participation. The last edition held in the same city two years ago saw eight teams compete.
By Olympics.com
05 November 2025 20:23 GMT+5
Twelve nations, including hosts India, defending champions Egypt and the runners-up from the last edition, Malaysia, will take part in the Squash World Cup 2025, to be held in Chennai from December 9 to 14.

World Squash, the global governing body of the sport, confirmed that 12 nations will compete in the fifth edition of the mixed-team tournament at the Express Avenue Mall.

The previous edition, held at the same venue in 2023, featured eight squash teams. It was the first Squash World Cup in 12 years.
Squash World Cup 2025 teams
Australia, Brazil, Egypt, Hong Kong China, India, Islamic Republic of Iran, Japan, Malaysia, Poland, Republic of Korea, South Africa, Switzerland

From Asia, hosts India will be joined by Iran, Japan, Malaysia, South Korea and Hong Kong China.

Africa will be represented by Egypt and South Africa while Poland and Switzerland will be the two European teams at the competition. Australia and Brazil will represent Oceania and Panamericana, respectively.

Each team will comprise two men and two women, with ties consisting of four best-of-five-game matches. The format introduced in 2023 - games played to seven points with sudden-death tiebreaks at 6-6 - will continue this year.

At the last edition, in 2023, Egypt claimed the title in an exciting come-from-behind victory against Malaysia. India were beaten by Malaysia in the semi-finals.

The Squash World Cup was first held in 1996 in Petaling Jaya, Malaysia, where Australia emerged as champions.

England won the second edition in 1999 in Hertogenbosch, Netherlands, before Egypt triumphed in the third in 2011, also in Chennai.

Egypt with two triumphs are the most successful team in Squash World Cup history. India’s third-place finish in 2023 is their best finish at a Squash World Cup.
