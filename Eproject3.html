Saina Nehwal backs PV Sindhu to win a third Olympic medal at LA 2028
The London 2012 bronze medallist added that Lakshya Sen, HS Prannoy and Satwik-Chirag remain big hopes but fitness and form would be key.
By Soumitra Bose
06 November 2025 13:00 GMT+5
London 2012 Olympics medallist Saina Nehwal feels PV Sindhu has the “experience” and hunger to go for a third Olympic medal in badminton.

On the sidelines of the Fit India National Fitness and Wellness Conclave held in Mumbai earlier this week, the former world No. 1 told Olympics.com that Sindhu’s chances depended on “how well she managed her body and form closer to the LA 2028 Olympics.”

Having won a medal each at Rio 2016 and Tokyo 2020, India returned empty-handed from Paris 2024. Lakshya Sen, in men’s singles, and the doubles pair of Satwiksairaj Rankireddy and Chirag Shetty came close to a podium finish but only just at the Paris Games.

Saina feels Sen, HS Prannoy and Satwik-Chirag will be India’s best hopes once again but “it was too early to predict anything since fitness and form are never guaranteed and what matters most are the weeks before the final cut is made for Games qualification.”

At Tokyo 2020, former world champion Sindhu became the first Indian woman and only the second Indian athlete - after Sushil Kumar - to win two individual Olympic medals.

Sindhu, who won silver in Rio 2016, beat People’s Republic of China’s He Bingjiao 21-13, 21-15 to win the bronze medal in the women’s singles. In Paris, Sindhu lost to the same opponent 21-19, 21-14 at the pre-quarterfinals stage.

Last month, Sindhu withdrew from all Badminton World Federation Tour events in 2025 due to a foot injury she sustained before the European leg.

The 30-year-old is currently world No. 14 and could drop further. Sindhu in a social media post said she was not counting on her support staff to bounce back in the new year.

In 2025, Sindhu’s form has been rather tepid. At least half a dozen first-round exits have not done her reputation any good. Injuries have bothered her in the last couple of seasons.

“Injuries are bound to happen,” said Saina. “And when you push yourself hard against younger opponents, the risks are higher.

“Sindhu plays the big tournaments very well and if she can manage her stress levels, recovery and plan well in the next three years, India can surely look at a medal from her.”
